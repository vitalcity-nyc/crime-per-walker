Outdoor felonies / pedestrian volume / New York City

Crime per walker, not crime per resident

Conventional crime maps divide incidents by the people who live in a neighborhood. That punishes commercial corridors where the residential population is tiny relative to the foot traffic. This map uses an MIT pedestrian-flow model as the denominator instead — outdoor incidents per million pedestrian-hours on the same two-hour window that the model estimates. Three years of NYPD data (2022–2024), 197 New York City neighborhoods, six time slots, and seven crime categories — robbery, felony assault, grand larceny, misdemeanor assault, petit larceny, a violent-crime composite, and all felonies — viewable separately or summed.

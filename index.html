Outdoor felonies / pedestrian volume / New York City

Crime per walker, not crime per resident

Conventional crime maps divide incidents by the people who live in a neighborhood. That punishes commercial corridors where the residential population is tiny relative to the foot traffic. This map uses an MIT pedestrian-flow model as the denominator instead — outdoor incidents per million pedestrian-hours on the same window that the model estimates. Three years of NYPD data (2022–2024), at neighborhood or census-tract resolution, six daytime windows that the model covers (8a–7p) plus evening and late-night raw-count views for after dark.

Re-ranking

The map most New York City reads gets these neighborhoods wrong

Each dot is a neighborhood. The horizontal axis is its rank when felonies are divided by residents (the standard map). The vertical axis is its rank when felonies are divided by walkers (this map). Dots on the gray diagonal rank the same either way. Dots below the line look worse per resident than they really are per walker — these are the commercial corridors the standard map penalizes for being busy. Dots above the line are the opposite — residential neighborhoods where each walker faces more risk than the per-resident rate suggests.

Borough Bronx Manhattan Brooklyn Queens Staten Island

How to read it. Felonies-only (all hours of 2022–2024) divided by population vs. divided by modeled weekday-PM walker-hours. Population: 2010 census tracts via CDC PLACES, mapped to 2020 NTAs (covers ~93 % of residents); NTAs with fewer than 1,000 residents or zero felonies are dropped. Hover any dot for details.

Sources. Outdoor felony complaints: NYPD Complaint Data Historic, filtered to LAW_CAT_CD = FELONY and premise types STREET, PARK/PLAYGROUND, HIGHWAY/PARKWAY, BUS STOP, BRIDGE. Pedestrian volume: Sevtsuk, A., Basu, R., et al., Estimating Pedestrian Flows on Street Networks in New York City, Nature Cities, 2025, using the NYC Pedestrian Network Estimates dataset (2018–2019). Neighborhood boundaries: 2020 NTAs from NYC DCP. Full methodology: METHODOLOGY.md. Subway felonies and bus-interior crime are excluded because the pedestrian-flow model only measures above-ground walking. Basemap © CARTO / OpenStreetMap.
